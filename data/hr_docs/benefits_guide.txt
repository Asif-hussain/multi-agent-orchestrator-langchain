DETAILED HEALTH PLAN COMPARISONS

Choosing the Right Medical Plan
When selecting your medical plan, consider:
- Your expected healthcare usage (frequent visits vs. occasional)
- Whether you have ongoing prescriptions or chronic conditions
- Your preferred doctors and whether they're in-network
- Your risk tolerance for out-of-pocket costs
- Whether you want to save with an HSA

PPO Gold Plan - Best for:
- Frequent doctor visits
- Ongoing medications or treatments
- Families with children
- Those who want predictable copays
- Lower out-of-pocket maximums

PPO Silver Plan - Best for:
- Moderate healthcare usage
- Generally healthy individuals
- Those wanting balance between premium and coverage
- Mid-range deductible comfort level

HDHP with HSA - Best for:
- Healthy individuals with minimal healthcare needs
- Those wanting to save on premiums
- People interested in HSA tax advantages
- High earners wanting additional tax-deferred savings
- Those comfortable with higher deductibles

Health Savings Account (HSA) Details
If you choose the HDHP plan, you gain access to an HSA:

Triple Tax Advantage:
1. Contributions are pre-tax (reduce taxable income)
2. Growth is tax-free (investment earnings)
3. Withdrawals are tax-free (for qualified medical expenses)

HSA Contribution Limits (2024):
- Individual coverage: $4,150 per year
- Family coverage: $8,300 per year
- Catch-up (age 55+): Additional $1,000
- Employer contributes $1,000/$2,000 automatically

HSA Investment Options:
- Funds over $1,000 can be invested in mutual funds
- Long-term wealth building potential
- Funds roll over year to year (never expire)
- Portable - stays with you if you leave company

Qualified HSA Expenses:
- Medical, dental, vision copays and deductibles
- Prescription medications
- Medical equipment and supplies
- Mental health services
- Chiropractic care
- Acupuncture
- Physical therapy
- Over-the-counter medications (with prescription)
- COBRA premiums
- Long-term care insurance premiums
- Medicare premiums (after age 65)

401(k) RETIREMENT PLAN DETAILS

Investment Options
Your 401(k) offers diverse investment choices:

Target-Date Funds (Recommended for most employees):
- Automatically adjust asset allocation as you near retirement
- Example: Target Date 2055 Fund for someone retiring in 2055
- Diversified across stocks, bonds, and other assets
- Low management fees (0.08% expense ratio)

Index Funds:
- S&P 500 Index Fund: Large-cap U.S. stocks
- Total Stock Market Index: Broad U.S. market exposure
- International Stock Index: Developed markets outside U.S.
- Bond Index Fund: U.S. investment-grade bonds
- Low expense ratios (0.04-0.15%)

Actively Managed Funds:
- Growth funds focusing on appreciation
- Value funds focusing on undervalued stocks
- Small-cap and mid-cap options
- Sector-specific funds (technology, healthcare, etc.)
- Higher expense ratios (0.50-1.00%)

Company Stock:
- Option to invest in company shares
- Limited to 20% of total portfolio (for diversification)
- Quarterly company match may be in company stock

Making Contribution Changes:
- Log into 401(k) portal at Fidelity.com
- Changes can be made anytime
- Effective on next pay period
- Increase contributions by 1% annually (auto-escalation)
- Contribution limits apply across all employer 401(k) plans

Employer Match Details:
Company matches 100% of your first 5% contribution:
- Example: $60,000 salary, 5% contribution = $3,000
- Company match: $3,000 (100% of your contribution)
- Total annual retirement savings: $6,000

Vesting Schedule (Employer Match):
- Year 1: 0% vested
- Year 2: 33% vested
- Year 3: 66% vested
- Year 4+: 100% vested
- Your own contributions: Always 100% vested immediately

Roth 401(k) Option:
Contribute after-tax dollars, withdraw tax-free in retirement:
- Good for younger employees expecting higher future tax rates
- No income limits (unlike Roth IRA)
- Can contribute to both Traditional and Roth 401(k)
- Same contribution limits apply combined

Loans and Hardship Withdrawals:
401(k) Loan:
- Borrow up to 50% of vested balance (max $50,000)
- Must repay with interest within 5 years
- Repayment through payroll deduction
- Interest paid back to your own account
- Consider carefully - reduces retirement savings

Hardship Withdrawal:
- Available for immediate financial need
- Medical expenses, foreclosure prevention, education costs
- Subject to income tax and 10% penalty if under age 59Â½
- Cannot be repaid
- Must exhaust other options first

Life Insurance Coverage Details:
Basic Life Insurance (Company-Paid):
- Coverage amount: 2x annual salary
- Maximum coverage: $500,000

Voluntary Supplemental Life Insurance (Employee-Paid):
- Coverage options: 1x to 5x annual salary

- Medical underwriting may be required over $300,000
- Spouse coverage: Up to $100,000
- Child coverage: $10,000 per child
- Premiums based on age and tobacco use

Disability Insurance Details:
Short-Term Disability (STD):
- Benefit: 60% of base salary
- Maximum weekly benefit: $2,000
- Waiting period: 14 days
- Benefit period: Up to 180 days
- Covers illness, injury, pregnancy, recovery from surgery
- Must be unable to perform your own occupation

Long-Term Disability (LTD):
- Benefit: 60% of base salary
- Maximum monthly benefit: $10,000
- Waiting period: 180 days (when STD ends)
- Benefit duration: Until age 65 or recovery
- Own-occupation for first 2 years
- Any-occupation after 2 years
- Includes rehabilitation and return-to-work support

Filing a Disability Claim:
1. Notify your manager and HR immediately
2. Obtain physician's statement of disability
3. Complete claim forms (available from HR)
4. Submit within 30 days of disability start
5. Claims processed within 15 business days
6. Benefit payments begin after waiting period ends

BENEFITS ENROLLMENT GUIDE

New Hire Enrollment (First 30 Days):
Within 30 days of hire date, you must:
1. Log into HR portal (hr.company.com)
2. Review all benefits options
3. Make your selections:
   - Medical plan choice (or waive if other coverage)
   - Dental plan (automatic unless waived)
   - Vision plan enrollment (optional)
   - 401(k) contribution election
   - Life insurance beneficiary designation
   - FSA elections (if applicable)
4. Submit enrollment
5. Coverage effective first of month after hire date

If you don't enroll within 30 days:
- You'll be enrolled in default medical plan (PPO Silver)
- Dental coverage automatic
- No vision coverage
- No FSA elections
- Can't make changes until next open enrollment (except qualifying life events)

Annual Open Enrollment (November):
Each year, you can:
- Change medical plans
- Adjust 401(k) contributions
- Enroll or drop voluntary benefits
- Update FSA elections
- Review and update beneficiaries
- Changes effective January 1

Qualifying Life Events:
You can make changes within 30 days of:
- Marriage or divorce
- Birth or adoption of child
- Death of spouse or dependent
- Spouse's loss of coverage
- Significant change in spouse's employment
- Change to full-time or part-time status
- Loss of Medicaid/CHIP eligibility

Required Documentation:
- Marriage: Marriage certificate
- Birth: Birth certificate
- Adoption: Adoption decree
- Divorce: Divorce decree
- Loss of coverage: Letter from other insurance
- Employment change: Spouse's employment letter
