NEW EMPLOYEE ONBOARDING GUIDE

WELCOME TO THE TEAM!
This guide will help you navigate your first weeks at the company.

WEEK 1: GETTING STARTED

Day 1 - Orientation
- Arrive at 9:00 AM at the main reception desk
- HR will provide your employee badge, laptop, and welcome packet
- Complete new hire paperwork including I-9, W-4, and direct deposit forms
- Set up benefits enrollment in the HR portal within first 30 days
- IT will set up your email, Slack, and system access
- Meet with your manager for role overview and expectations

Day 2-5 - Initial Training
- Complete mandatory compliance training modules (4 hours)
- Shadow team members to understand workflows
- Set up 1-on-1 meetings with key stakeholders
- Review team documentation and processes
- Begin accessing systems and tools needed for your role

WEEK 2-4: INTEGRATION

Training and Development
- Complete department-specific training
- Attend team meetings and stand-ups
- Begin working on starter projects with guidance
- Schedule coffee chats with cross-functional team members
- Complete product knowledge training

30-Day Check-in
- Meet with HR for benefits questions and onboarding feedback
- First check-in with manager on progress and questions
- Ensure all compliance training is completed
- Confirm access to all necessary systems

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION
Please bring the following on your first day:
- Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport)
- Social Security card or birth certificate
- Void check or bank routing information for direct deposit
- Emergency contact information

□ Complete I-9 employment eligibility verification

□ Join relevant Slack channels and team meetings
□ Schedule recurring 1-on-1 with manager

COMMON QUESTIONS

Q: When do I get paid?
A: Employees are paid bi-weekly on Fridays. Your first paycheck will be processed on the next pay cycle after your start date.

Q: How do I request time off?
A: Submit PTO requests through the HR portal at least 2 weeks in advance.

Q: What are the working hours?
A: Core hours are 9 AM - 5 PM. Many teams offer flexible start/end times. Discuss with your manager.

Q: Where do I park?
A: Employee parking is available in the garage. Your badge provides access. Visitor parking is in the front lot.

Q: What if I'm sick?
A: Notify your manager as early as possible. Submit sick leave through the HR portal.

PROBATIONARY PERIOD
The first 90 days constitute a probationary period during which performance is closely evaluated. At the end of this period, you'll have a formal review with your manager.

We're excited to have you on the team! If you have any questions during onboarding, don't hesitate to reach out to HR or your manager.

MONTH 2-3: BUILDING MOMENTUM

60-Day Milestone
By day 60, you should be:
- Independently managing assigned tasks and projects
- Contributing actively in team meetings
- Understanding your role's impact on company goals
- Building relationships across departments
- Identifying areas for improvement or innovation
- Comfortable with all core tools and systems

Manager Check-in Topics:
- Progress on initial goals and objectives
- Clarity on role responsibilities and expectations
- Training needs or skill gaps
- Team integration and collaboration
- Workload and work-life balance
- Career development interests

90-Day Performance Review
Your 90-day review will assess:
- Technical competency and job knowledge
- Quality and timeliness of work
- Communication and collaboration skills
- Alignment with company values
- Initiative and problem-solving abilities
- Attendance and punctuality

Successful completion of the probationary period results in:
- Confirmation of employment status
- Eligibility for performance bonuses (if applicable)
- Full access to all employee programs
- Consideration for additional responsibilities

COMMUNICATION CHANNELS AND ETIQUETTE

Slack Guidelines:
- Set your status when away, in meetings, or on PTO
- Use threads to keep conversations organized
- @mention only when you need someone's immediate attention
- Use channels for team communication, DMs for private matters
- Keep #general for company-wide announcements only
- Mute channels that aren't relevant to your work
- Respect do-not-disturb hours (typically 6 PM - 8 AM)

Email Best Practices:
- Check email at least twice daily during business hours
- Respond to urgent requests within 4 hours
- Use clear, descriptive subject lines
- Include relevant stakeholders in CC
- Set out-of-office replies when on PTO
- Keep messages professional and concise
- Use calendar invites for all meetings

Meeting Culture:
- Arrive on time (or 2 minutes early for video calls)
- Send agendas in advance when hosting
- Take notes and share action items afterward
- Mute when not speaking on large calls
- Turn on video for small team meetings when possible
- Respect meeting-free focus time (typically 9-11 AM)
- Decline meetings you don't need to attend

PERFORMANCE EXPECTATIONS AND GOALS

Goal Setting Framework:
We use the OKR (Objectives and Key Results) methodology:
- Objectives: Qualitative goals that inspire and challenge
- Key Results: Quantitative metrics that measure success
- Quarterly planning cycles with monthly check-ins
- Individual goals aligned with team and company objectives

Example OKR Structure:
Objective: Improve customer onboarding experience
- Key Result 1: Reduce time-to-first-value from 14 to 7 days
- Key Result 2: Increase onboarding completion rate to 90%
- Key Result 3: Achieve NPS score of 50+ for new customers

Your First Quarter Goals:
Your manager will work with you to set realistic 90-day goals that focus on:
- Learning and development
- Building key relationships
- Delivering initial projects
- Understanding company processes
- Demonstrating core competencies

Performance Metrics:
Depending on your role, performance may be measured by:
- Project completion and quality
- Customer satisfaction scores
- Sales or revenue targets
- Code commits and reviews (engineering)
- Campaign performance (marketing)
- Response times (support)
- Team collaboration feedback

CAREER GROWTH AND ADVANCEMENT

Career Ladder Framework:
Each department has defined career levels with clear expectations:
- Entry Level (I): Learning role fundamentals, guided work
- Mid Level (II): Independent execution, mentoring juniors
- Senior Level (III): Leading projects, influencing strategy
- Lead/Staff Level (IV): Organizational impact, thought leadership
- Principal/Director Level (V): Setting vision, cross-functional leadership

Promotion Process:
- Promotions typically occur during annual review cycle
- Based on demonstrated performance at next level for 6+ months
- Requires manager nomination and peer feedback
- Must meet technical and leadership criteria for new level
- Salary adjustments accompany promotions

Internal Mobility:
We encourage internal career movement:
- Internal job postings shared company-wide
- Employees can apply after 12 months in current role
- Informational interviews encouraged
- Internal transfers prioritized over external hires
- Must have manager conversation before applying
- Transfer requires current manager approval
