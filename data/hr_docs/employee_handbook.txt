EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK - HUMAN RESOURCES POLICIES

1. PAID TIME OFF (PTO) POLICY
All full-time employees accrue 15 days of PTO per year, starting from their hire date. PTO accrues at a rate of 1.25 days per month. Part-time employees accrue PTO on a pro-rated basis. PTO must be requested at least 2 weeks in advance for periods longer than 3 consecutive days. Employees can carry over up to 5 unused PTO days to the next calendar year. Any unused PTO beyond 5 days will be forfeited.

2. SICK LEAVE POLICY
Employees are entitled to 10 sick days per year. Sick leave does not roll over to the next year. For absences longer than 3 consecutive days, a doctor's note is required. Sick leave can be used for personal illness, medical appointments, or to care for immediate family members.

3. REMOTE WORK POLICY
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week with manager approval. Remote work requests must be submitted through the HR portal. Employees must maintain availability during core business hours (9 AM - 5 PM) and have reliable internet connectivity. Full-time remote positions require VP-level approval.

4. PERFORMANCE REVIEW PROCESS
Annual performance reviews are conducted in Q4 for all employees. Reviews assess performance against goals set at the beginning of the year. Mid-year check-ins occur in Q2. Performance ratings range from 1-5, with 3 being "meets expectations." Salary adjustments are determined based on performance ratings and budget availability.

5. EMPLOYEE BENEFITS
- Health Insurance: Company covers 80% of employee premiums, 60% for dependents
- Dental Insurance: Company covers 100% of employee premiums
- Vision Insurance: Available with employee contribution
- 401(k) Retirement Plan: Company matches up to 5% of salary
- Life Insurance: 2x annual salary provided at no cost
- Disability Insurance: Short-term and long-term disability coverage included

6. PARENTAL LEAVE POLICY
Primary caregivers receive 16 weeks of paid parental leave. Secondary caregivers receive 8 weeks of paid parental leave. Leave must be taken within 12 months of birth or adoption. Employees must notify HR at least 30 days before intended leave date.

7. PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT
Employees can request up to $2,000 annually for professional development, including courses, certifications, and conferences. Requests must be submitted through the learning management system and approved by the direct manager. Reimbursement requires proof of completion and expense receipts.

8. CODE OF CONDUCT
Employees must maintain professional behavior, respect colleagues, and adhere to company values. Harassment, discrimination, and retaliation are strictly prohibited. Violations should be reported to HR immediately. All reports are investigated confidentially.

9. DRESS CODE
Business casual attire is expected in the office. Client-facing employees should dress in business professional attire. Remote workers should maintain professional appearance for video calls.

10. TERMINATION AND RESIGNATION
Employees should provide at least 2 weeks notice upon resignation. Exit interviews are conducted by HR. Final paychecks include payment for unused PTO. Company property must be returned on the last day of employment.

11. WORKPLACE SAFETY AND SECURITY
All employees must comply with workplace safety regulations and report any hazards to facilities management immediately. Emergency evacuation procedures are posted in all common areas. Fire drills are conducted quarterly. Building access badges must be worn visibly at all times. Lost or stolen badges must be reported to security within 24 hours. Employees are prohibited from propping open secure doors or allowing unauthorized individuals to enter the building.

12. TECHNOLOGY USE AND DATA SECURITY
Company-provided equipment is for business use only. Personal use should be minimal and not interfere with work responsibilities. All company data must be stored on approved systems and encrypted when transmitted externally. Employees must use strong passwords (minimum 12 characters, mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols) and enable two-factor authentication on all company systems. Sharing passwords is strictly prohibited. Downloading unauthorized software or accessing inappropriate websites may result in disciplinary action.

13. SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY
Employees must not disclose confidential company information on social media platforms. When posting about work-related topics, employees must include a disclaimer that views are their own. Harassment or defamatory comments about colleagues, clients, or competitors are prohibited. Employees should be mindful that social media posts can impact the company's reputation.

14. EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT GUIDELINES
Business expenses must have a legitimate business purpose and be reasonable in amount. Meal expenses during business travel are reimbursed up to $75 per day. Hotel accommodations should not exceed $200 per night without prior approval. Flight bookings should be made in economy class; business class requires VP approval. All expenses must be submitted within 60 days with original receipts. Reimbursement processing takes 2-3 weeks from approval.

15. TIMEKEEPING AND ATTENDANCE
Non-exempt employees must accurately record all hours worked using the time tracking system. Time entries must be submitted by end-of-day each Friday. Falsifying time records is grounds for termination. Employees are expected to arrive on time and ready to work at their scheduled start time. Excessive tardiness (more than 3 instances per month) may result in disciplinary action. Unplanned absences must be reported to supervisors at least 2 hours before shift start.

16. WORKPLACE HARASSMENT PREVENTION
The company maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment of any kind. Harassment includes unwelcome conduct based on protected characteristics such as race, gender, age, religion, disability, or sexual orientation. Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature. All employees must complete annual harassment prevention training. Victims or witnesses of harassment should report incidents to HR immediately.

17. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests. Outside employment or business activities that compete with the company are prohibited without prior written approval. Employees must not accept gifts or entertainment from vendors or clients that could influence business decisions. All potential conflicts must be disclosed to HR and management.

18. CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-DISCLOSURE
Employees have access to confidential information including trade secrets, customer data, financial information, and business strategies. This information must not be shared with unauthorized individuals, including family and friends. Confidentiality obligations continue after employment ends. Breaching confidentiality may result in legal action and termination.

19. GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
Employees who have concerns or complaints should first discuss them with their direct supervisor. If the issue is not resolved or involves the supervisor, employees should contact HR. Formal grievances must be submitted in writing within 30 days of the incident. HR will investigate and respond within 15 business days. Retaliation against employees who file grievances is strictly prohibited.

20. EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY
The company provides equal employment opportunities to all employees and applicants without regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic. This policy applies to all terms and conditions of employment including recruitment, hiring, promotion, compensation, benefits, training, and termination. The company complies with all applicable federal, state, and local laws governing non-discrimination in employment.

21. DISABILITY ACCOMMODATIONS
The company is committed to providing reasonable accommodations to qualified employees with disabilities. Employees requiring accommodation should contact HR to initiate the interactive process. Documentation from a healthcare provider may be required to support the accommodation request. Accommodations are determined on a case-by-case basis and may include modified work schedules, assistive technology, or workspace modifications.

22. DRUG-FREE WORKPLACE
The company maintains a drug-free workplace policy. Use, possession, distribution, or being under the influence of illegal drugs or alcohol during work hours is prohibited. Employees may be subject to drug testing in accordance with applicable law. Violations may result in termination and referral to law enforcement. Employees struggling with substance abuse are encouraged to use the Employee Assistance Program.

23. EMPLOYEE ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (EAP)
The company provides confidential counseling services through our Employee Assistance Program. Services include mental health counseling, financial planning assistance, legal consultations, and work-life balance resources. Employees and immediate family members can access up to 6 free counseling sessions per year. EAP services are available 24/7 by phone or video conferencing. Using EAP services does not impact employment status or performance evaluations.

24. TUITION REIMBURSEMENT
Full-time employees with at least one year of service are eligible for tuition reimbursement up to $5,000 per year. Courses must be related to the employee's current job or career path within the company. Reimbursement requires a grade of B or higher. Employees must submit pre-approval forms before course enrollment. If employment ends within 12 months of reimbursement, employees must repay the reimbursement amount.

25. WELLNESS PROGRAM
The company offers a comprehensive wellness program including gym membership reimbursement up to $50/month, annual health screenings, flu shots, and wellness challenges. Employees can earn wellness points by participating in activities, which can be redeemed for rewards. The program includes mental health resources, nutritional counseling, and smoking cessation support.
