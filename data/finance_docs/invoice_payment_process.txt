INVOICE AND PAYMENT PROCESSING GUIDE

ACCOUNTS PAYABLE OVERVIEW

The Accounts Payable (AP) department is responsible for processing vendor invoices, managing payment schedules, and maintaining vendor relationships. This guide explains how to submit invoices, payment timelines, and troubleshooting common issues.

Contact Information:
- Email: ap@company.com
- Phone: Extension 7200
- Office: Finance Department, Building B, 3rd Floor
- System: NetSuite ERP at erp.company.com

INVOICE SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS

Required Information

All invoices must include:
- Vendor legal name and address
- Vendor tax ID (W-9 on file)
- Invoice number (unique)
- Invoice date
- Payment terms (Net 30, Net 60, etc.)
- Purchase Order (PO) number (if applicable)
- Itemized list of goods/services
- Quantities and unit prices
- Subtotal, taxes, and total amount
- Remittance address
- Payment method preferences

Missing Information:
- Invoices with missing required information will be rejected
- Vendor will be contacted to resubmit complete invoice
- Processing delayed until complete documentation received

Submission Methods

Electronic Submission (Preferred):
- Email: ap@company.com
- Subject line: "Invoice [Invoice Number] - [Vendor Name]"
- Attach PDF of invoice
- Include PO number in email body if applicable

Vendor Portal:
- Access at: vendors.company.com
- Upload invoice directly
- Track approval status
- View payment history
- Faster processing time (1-2 days vs 3-5 days for email)

Mail:
- Company Name
- Accounts Payable Department
- 123 Business Street
- City, State 12345
- Processing time: 5-7 business days

PURCHASE ORDERS (PO)

When PO is Required

Purchase orders are mandatory for:
- All purchases over $1,000
- Software licenses and subscriptions
- Consulting services
- Equipment and hardware
- Recurring services

Exceptions (no PO required):
- Utilities
- Rent
- Employee reimbursements
- Pre-approved vendor agreements
- Credit card purchases under $500

Creating a Purchase Order

Process:
1. Log into NetSuite ERP
2. Navigate to Purchasing > Create Purchase Order
3. Select vendor from approved vendor list
4. Enter line items with descriptions
5. Attach quote or proposal
6. Add GL account codes
7. Submit for approval

Approval Workflow:
- Under $5,000: Manager approval
- $5,000 - $25,000: Director approval
- $25,000 - $100,000: VP approval
- Over $100,000: CFO approval

Three-Way Match:
Invoices are matched against:
1. Purchase Order (PO)
2. Goods Receipt/Service Confirmation
3. Invoice

All three must match for automatic approval. Discrepancies require review and resolution.

VENDOR MANAGEMENT

New Vendor Setup

Required Documents:
- W-9 form (for US vendors) or W-8 (for international vendors)
- ACH authorization form (for electronic payments)
- Certificate of Insurance (if applicable)
- Vendor information form
- Banking details for wire transfers

Setup Process:
1. Submit new vendor request through NetSuite
2. Attach all required documents
3. Procurement review (2-3 business days)
4. Compliance and background check
5. Vendor created in system
6. Confirmation email sent to requestor

Processing Time:
- Domestic vendors: 5-7 business days
- International vendors: 10-14 business days
- Expedited setup available for urgent needs (requires VP approval)

Vendor Classifications:
- Preferred Vendor: Pre-negotiated rates, faster processing
- Standard Vendor: Regular payment terms
- One-time Vendor: For single transactions, simplified setup

Vendor Information Updates:
- Banking changes: Submit updated ACH form
- Address changes: Email ap@company.com with documentation
- Tax ID changes: Submit new W-9
- Ownership changes: May require new vendor setup

PAYMENT TERMS AND SCHEDULES

Standard Payment Terms

Net 30: Payment due 30 days from invoice date (most common)
Net 60: Payment due 60 days from invoice date
Net 15: Payment due 15 days from invoice date
Due on Receipt: Payment due immediately
2/10 Net 30: 2% discount if paid within 10 days, otherwise net 30

Payment Processing Schedule:
- Check runs: Weekly on Thursdays
- ACH payments: Bi-weekly on Tuesdays and Thursdays
- Wire transfers: Daily (for urgent payments)
- International wires: 2-3 business days processing

Payment Methods

ACH (Automated Clearing House) - Preferred:
- No transaction fees
- 1-2 business days to vendor account
- Secure and trackable
- Requires vendor ACH authorization

Wire Transfer:
- Same-day or next-day processing
- Fees: $15 domestic, $45 international
- Used for urgent payments or large amounts
- Requires additional approval for amounts over $50,000

Check:
- Mailed within 1 business day of check run
- Delivery time: 3-5 business days USPS
- Used when ACH not available
- Can request check pick-up from AP office

Virtual Card:
- For select online vendors
- Immediate payment
- Enhanced security
- Earns cash-back rebates

INVOICE APPROVAL WORKFLOW

Standard Approval Process

Step 1: Invoice Receipt
- AP receives invoice
- Invoice logged in NetSuite
- Assigned invoice number
- Notification sent to approver

Step 2: Validation
- Verify vendor is in system
- Check for duplicate invoices
- Validate GL coding
- Match to PO if applicable
- Verify budget availability

Step 3: Manager Approval
- Manager receives email notification
- Reviews invoice details
- Approves or rejects with comments
- SLA: 3 business days

Step 4: AP Processing
- Final review for accuracy
- Schedule payment per terms
- Update vendor records
- Process payment on due date

Step 5: Payment Confirmation
- Payment issued
- Email notification to vendor
- Remittance advice sent
- Invoice marked paid in system

Approval Timeframes:
- PO-backed invoices: 1-2 business days
- Non-PO invoices under $1,000: 3-5 business days
- Non-PO invoices over $1,000: 5-7 business days
- Invoices with discrepancies: 7-10 business days

Expedited Processing:
- Available for urgent vendor needs
- Requires VP approval
- Rush fee may apply for wire transfers
- Must have legitimate business reason
- Submit at least 2 business days before needed

SPECIAL PAYMENT SCENARIOS

Recurring Payments

Subscription Services:
- Software licenses (monthly/annual)
- Cloud services (AWS, Azure, etc.)
- Professional memberships
- Utilities and facilities

Process:
- Initial invoice requires PO and approval
- Subsequent invoices auto-approved if amount matches
- Annual review of all recurring payments
- Notification if amount changes by >10%

Contract Payments:
- Based on milestone or schedule in contract
- Payment triggered by deliverable acceptance
- Requires project manager confirmation
- May involve retention (10% held until completion)

Retainer Agreements:
- Monthly invoices for fixed amount
- Auto-processed once agreement on file
- Annual renewal requires re-approval

International Payments

Additional Requirements:
- SWIFT code for wire transfers
- IBAN for European vendors
- Intermediary bank information
- Currency conversion rates
- Compliance documentation

Considerations:
- Foreign transaction fees apply
- Exchange rate fluctuations
- Longer processing time (3-5 business days)
- Tax withholding may be required
- W-8 form required for non-US vendors

Currency:
- Invoices can be in foreign currency
- Payment processed in invoice currency or USD
- Exchange rate locked at payment processing
- Rates available at erp.company.com/fx-rates

Employee Reimbursements

Different from vendor payments:
- Processed through expense system (Concur)
- Faster approval (1-2 days)
- Payment via direct deposit
- See Expense Reimbursement Policy for details

Should NOT submit through AP:
- Employee travel expenses
- Employee office supplies
- Employee professional development
- Employee mileage reimbursement

Credit Card Reconciliation

Corporate Card Payments:
- Invoices should NOT be submitted if already paid by corporate card
- Automatic payment to card issuer
- Employee must submit expense report
- Prevents duplicate payment

Procurement Card (P-Card):
- Department cards for small purchases
- Monthly reconciliation required
- Cardholder submits receipts and coding
- AP processes payment to card issuer

DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Invoice Discrepancies

Common Issues:
- Incorrect pricing
- Wrong quantities billed
- Services not rendered
- Duplicate invoices
- Missing purchase order
- Incorrect tax calculations

Resolution Process:
1. AP places invoice on hold
2. Notifies manager and vendor
3. Manager investigates discrepancy
4. Vendor submits corrected invoice or credit memo
5. Original invoice cancelled in system
6. Corrected invoice processed

Credit Memos:
- Issued by vendor for overcharges or returns
- Must reference original invoice
- Automatically applied against vendor balance
- Refund issued if no open invoices

Payment Disputes:
- Vendor claims non-payment
- AP provides payment proof (check copy, ACH trace)
- Research payment status
- Reissue payment if lost (stop payment on original check)

Late Payment Penalties:
- Generally not paid if invoice submitted late
- May be waived if internal processing delay
- Requires VP approval
- Documented in dispute log

COMPLIANCE AND AUDIT

Tax Compliance

Sales Tax:
- Vendors should charge applicable state/local sales tax
- Company exempt in some jurisdictions (exemption certificate on file)
- AP validates tax calculations
- Incorrect tax held for correction

Use Tax:
- Accrued on out-of-state purchases
- Calculated and remitted by Finance
- No action required from AP or managers

1099 Requirements:
- Applies to contractors and service providers
- Does NOT apply to corporations (with exceptions)
- Accurate W-9 forms required
- Penalties for non-compliance

Audit Trail:
- All invoice processing tracked
- Approval history maintained
- Payment records retained 7 years
- Electronic storage in NetSuite

SOX Compliance:
- Segregation of duties enforced
- Dual approval for large payments
- Regular access reviews
- Annual audit procedures

FRAUD PREVENTION

Security Measures

Payment Approval Limits:
- Prevents unauthorized large payments
- Escalation requirements enforced
- System controls cannot be overridden

Vendor Verification:
- Vendor changes verified by phone
- Banking changes require written authorization
- Suspicious requests investigated

Duplicate Detection:
- System flags duplicate invoice numbers
- Same vendor and amount flagged
- Prevents accidental double-payment

Common Fraud Schemes:
- Fake vendor invoices
- Altered banking information
- Invoice amount inflation
- Phantom vendors
- Kickback schemes

Reporting Suspicious Activity:
- Contact AP Manager immediately
- Email: fraud@company.com
- Anonymous hotline: 1-800-ETHICS-1
- All reports investigated confidentially

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: How long does payment take?
A: Standard Net 30 invoices are paid 30 days from invoice date. ACH payments take 1-2 days, checks take 3-5 days to arrive.

Q: Why was my invoice rejected?
A: Common reasons: missing PO, incomplete information, duplicate invoice, pricing discrepancy. Check email for specific reason.

Q: Can I get paid faster?
A: Early payment available for invoices offering quick-pay discounts (e.g., 2/10 Net 30). Otherwise, standard terms apply.

Q: How do I check payment status?
A: Vendors can check at vendors.company.com. Internal employees can check NetSuite or email ap@company.com.

Q: What if I didn't receive payment?
A: Contact ap@company.com with invoice number. We'll provide payment trace and reissue if needed.

Q: How do I update my banking information?
A: Submit new ACH authorization form to ap@company.com. Allow 5-7 days for update.

Q: Can I get a payment confirmation?
A: Yes, remittance advice is emailed automatically. Contact AP for additional confirmation if needed.

Q: What if the invoice amount changed from the PO?
A: Submit explanation with invoice. Manager approval required for variances over 10%.
