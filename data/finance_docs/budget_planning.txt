ANNUAL BUDGET PLANNING AND MANAGEMENT GUIDE

BUDGET OVERVIEW

The annual budget process establishes financial targets, allocates resources, and provides a framework for financial decision-making throughout the fiscal year. This guide explains the budget planning cycle, departmental responsibilities, and ongoing budget management.

Fiscal Year: January 1 - December 31
Budget Planning Cycle: August - November
Budget Approval: December Board Meeting
Budget Effective: January 1

Contact Information:
- Budget Planning: budgets@company.com
- FP&A Team: fpa@company.com
- Budget System: Adaptive Insights at budgets.company.com

BUDGET PLANNING TIMELINE

August: Strategic Planning
- Review company strategic objectives
- Analyze prior year performance
- Identify growth initiatives
- Preliminary revenue forecasts
- Headcount planning assumptions

September: Departmental Budget Requests

Week 1-2: Budget kickoff
- Finance distributes budget templates
- Training sessions for budget owners
- Review budget guidelines and assumptions
- Q&A sessions scheduled

Week 3-4: Departmental submissions
- Department heads submit initial budget requests
- Include headcount, programs, capital expenses
- Justify new initiatives and increases
- Submit via Adaptive Insights platform

October: Review and Consolidation

Week 1-2: Finance review
- FP&A team consolidates departmental budgets
- Identify gaps vs. revenue targets
- Analyze cross-departmental dependencies
- Prepare questions and challenges

Week 3-4: Budget review meetings
- One-on-one meetings with each department head
- Discuss variances and justifications
- Negotiate priorities and trade-offs
- Document decisions and revisions

November: Finalization

Week 1-2: Revised submissions
- Departments submit revised budgets
- Incorporate feedback from review meetings
- Final adjustments and prioritization
- Board presentation preparation

Week 3-4: Executive review
- CFO presents consolidated budget to CEO
- Executive team final approval
- Board presentation prepared
- Communication plan finalized

December: Approval and Communication

Week 1-2: Board approval
- Present to Board of Directors
- Address board questions
- Board vote for approval
- Final budget locked in system

Week 3-4: Communication
- Departmental budget allocations communicated
- Budget targets loaded into financial systems
- Training on budget management
- Year-end close preparation

BUDGET COMPONENTS

Operating Expenses (OpEx)

Personnel Costs (typically 60-70% of budget):
- Salaries and wages
- Benefits (health, dental, vision, 401k match)
- Payroll taxes (FICA, unemployment)
- Bonuses and commissions
- Stock-based compensation
- Contractor and temporary staff
- Recruiting and hiring costs

Non-Personnel Operating Costs:
- Office rent and facilities
- Utilities (electric, water, internet)
- Office supplies and equipment
- Software licenses and subscriptions
- Professional services (legal, accounting, consulting)
- Marketing and advertising
- Travel and entertainment
- Training and development
- Insurance
- Depreciation and amortization

Departmental Budgets:
- Each department receives allocation
- Department head manages within allocation
- Variances tracked monthly
- Reallocations require approval

Capital Expenditures (CapEx)

Definition: Purchases over $5,000 with useful life >1 year

Categories:
- Computer equipment and servers
- Office furniture and fixtures
- Leasehold improvements
- Vehicles
- Manufacturing equipment
- Software development (capitalized)

Approval Process:
- Submit capital request form
- Include business case and ROI analysis
- Requires VP approval
- CFO approval for items >$25,000
- Board approval for items >$100,000

Depreciation:
- Computer equipment: 3-year straight-line
- Furniture and fixtures: 7-year straight-line
- Leasehold improvements: Over lease term
- Software: 3-5 year straight-line

Revenue Budget

Components:
- Product sales revenue
- Service revenue
- Subscription/recurring revenue
- Professional services
- Other income

Forecasting Methodology:
- Sales pipeline analysis
- Historical trends
- Market analysis
- New product launches
- Pricing changes
- Customer retention assumptions

Ownership:
- Sales team provides pipeline data
- Product team provides product roadmap
- Marketing provides lead generation targets
- Finance consolidates and risk-adjusts

Headcount Planning

Headcount Budget Process:
1. Department identifies staffing needs
2. Submits headcount request with:
   - Role title and level
   - Hire date (by quarter)
   - Fully-loaded cost estimate
   - Business justification
   - Impact if not approved
3. HR validates compensation ranges
4. Finance reviews affordability
5. Executive team approves priority roles

Fully-Loaded Cost Calculation:
- Base salary
- Benefits: +30% of base
- Payroll taxes: +8% of base
- Stock compensation: Per equity plan
- Bonus: Per compensation plan
- Recruiting cost: $10K-$30K per role
- Onboarding and training: $5K per employee

Example:
Software Engineer, Senior Level
- Base salary: $150,000
- Benefits (30%): $45,000
- Payroll tax (8%): $12,000
- Bonus (15%): $22,500
- Stock/RSUs: $40,000
- Fully-loaded annual cost: $269,500

Backfill vs. New Headcount:
- Backfills: Replacing departures, generally auto-approved
- New headcount: Incremental growth, requires justification
- Budget includes turnover assumption (10-15%)

BUDGET ASSUMPTIONS

Standard Planning Assumptions

Inflation and Cost Increases:
- Salary increases: 3-5% merit pool
- Benefits inflation: 5-7% annual
- Software/SaaS: 5-10% annual increases
- Office rent: Per lease agreement
- Utilities: 2-3% inflation

Growth Assumptions:
- Revenue growth target: Set by executive team
- Headcount growth: Aligned with revenue
- Customer acquisition: Marketing and sales plans
- Retention rate: Historical trends
- Market expansion: Strategic initiatives

Macro-Economic Factors:
- Interest rates: For cash investments and debt
- Exchange rates: For international operations
- Tax rates: Current federal, state, local rates
- Economic outlook: Recession, growth, stability

Scenario Planning:
- Base case: Most likely scenario (80% probability)
- Upside case: Optimistic scenario (growth +20%)
- Downside case: Conservative scenario (growth -20%)
- Enables contingency planning

BUDGET MANAGEMENT

Monthly Budget Reviews

Variance Analysis:
- Actual vs. budget comparison
- Identify variances >10% or >$10,000
- Investigate root causes
- Document explanations
- Plan corrective actions

Variance Categories:
- Favorable: Actual < Budget for expenses, Actual > Budget for revenue
- Unfavorable: Actual > Budget for expenses, Actual < Budget for revenue

Common Variance Drivers:
- Timing differences (delayed hiring, project delays)
- One-time expenses not budgeted
- Revenue shortfalls or windfalls
- Market changes
- Strategic pivots

Budget Reforecasting:
- Quarterly reforecasts (Q1, Q2, Q3)
- Update assumptions based on actuals
- Adjust full-year outlook
- Communicate changes to stakeholders
- Board updates

Budget vs. Forecast:
- Budget: Original annual plan (fixed)
- Forecast: Updated projection (changes quarterly)
- Both tracked for accountability

Budget Transfers and Reallocations

Transferring Budget Between Categories:

Within Department:
- Manager discretion for transfers <$5,000
- Finance approval for transfers >$5,000
- Document business justification
- Update in budgeting system

Between Departments:
- Requires both department heads' approval
- Finance review and documentation
- CFO approval for amounts >$25,000
- Permanent vs. one-time transfer designation

Process:
1. Submit budget transfer request form
2. Include source and destination GL accounts
3. Provide business justification
4. Obtain required approvals
5. Finance processes in system
6. Confirmation email sent

Common Scenarios:
- Project budget moves with transferred employee
- Shared service cost reallocations
- Centralized software moved to departmental budget
- Marketing campaign budget adjustments

Budget Augmentations:
- Increasing total budget (rare)
- Requires CFO approval
- Must identify funding source
- Board notification if material

Hiring Freeze or Budget Cuts

Triggers:
- Revenue shortfall >15%
- Economic downturn
- Strategic pivot
- Cash flow concerns

Levels of Cost Reduction:

Level 1: Soft Freeze (most common)
- Hiring slowed, not stopped
- Only critical roles approved
- Discretionary spending reduced
- Travel restrictions
- Target: 10-15% reduction

Level 2: Hard Freeze
- All hiring stopped except executive-approved
- All non-essential spending paused
- Contract renewals reviewed
- Events and conferences cancelled
- Target: 20-30% reduction

Level 3: Layoffs/Restructuring
- Workforce reduction
- Department consolidations
- Product/service line elimination
- Office closures
- Target: 30%+ reduction

Budget Flexibility:
- 10% budget buffer held by finance
- Released quarterly based on performance
- Enables investment in high-priority opportunities
- Cushion for unexpected needs

CAPITAL BUDGETING

Capital Expenditure Approval

Capital Budget Allocation:
- Total capital budget set annually
- Allocated to departments or held centrally
- Multi-year projects tracked separately
- Unused capital does not roll over

Request Process:
1. Complete capital expenditure request form
2. Include:
   - Description of asset
   - Business justification
   - Cost estimate
   - Vendor quotes
   - Alternative options considered
   - ROI calculation
   - Implementation timeline
3. Submit for approval per authorization matrix
4. Finance reviews and approves/denies
5. If approved, PO issued and tracked

ROI Requirements:
- Required for discretionary capital items
- Payback period <3 years preferred
- NPV analysis for large projects
- Include ongoing operating costs

Leasing vs. Buying:
- Lease if <3 year useful life
- Buy if long-term asset
- Compare total cost of ownership
- Consider technology obsolescence
- Tax implications reviewed by finance

PROJECT BUDGETING

Project-Specific Budgets

Types of Projects:
- New product development

- System implementations

- M&A integration
- Strategic initiatives

Project Budget Components:
- Personnel (internal team time)

- Technology and tools
- Materials and supplies
- Travel
- Overhead allocation

Project Approval:
- Submit project charter and budget
- Include scope, timeline, resources

Tracking:
- Dedicated GL codes for projects
- Monthly burn rate tracking
- Variance to budget monitored
- Change requests for scope/budget changes
- Post-project review of actuals vs. budget

BUDGET BEST PRACTICES

Planning Best Practices

Zero-Based Budgeting:
- Justify all expenses from zero
- Don't assume prior year budget is baseline
- Challenge existing spend
- Prioritize initiatives
- Forces efficiency

Driver-Based Budgeting:
- Identify key business drivers
- Link expenses to drivers
- Examples:
  * Support costs per customer
  * Sales expenses per rep
  * Marketing cost per lead
  * IT cost per employee
- Enables scenario modeling

Bottom-Up + Top-Down:
- Departments build bottom-up detailed budgets
- Executive team sets top-down targets
- Iterate to alignment
- Balances realism with ambition

Contingency Planning:
- 5-10% budget reserve for unknowns
- Risk assessment and mitigation plans
- Scenario planning
- Flexibility built in

Management Best Practices

Monthly Reviews:
- Review actuals by 5th business day
- Variance analysis by 10th business day
- Reforecasting quarterly
- Proactive communication

Budget Ownership:
- Budget owners accountable for results
- Regular review meetings
- Documented decisions
- Performance tied to budget management

Cost Controls:
- Approval workflows enforced
- Vendor negotiations
- Contract reviews before renewal
- Eliminate unused subscriptions
- Consolidate vendors

Transparency:
- Budgets accessible to appropriate levels
- Clear communication of targets
- Regular updates on company performance
- Financial literacy training

BUDGET COMPLIANCE

Policies and Controls

Budget Authority:
- Managers can approve spend within budget
- Over-budget requires next level approval
- Automatic PO blocking if budget exceeded
- Budget transfer required before spending

Approval Matrix:
- Under $1,000: Manager
- $1,000 - $10,000: Director
- $10,000 - $50,000: VP
- Over $50,000: CFO
- Over $250,000: CEO

Audit and Compliance:
- Internal audit reviews budget process annually
- Sampling of expenses for compliance
- Manager attestations quarterly
- SOX controls for financial reporting

Violations and Consequences:
- Over-budget spending without approval
- Budget transfers without documentation
- Circumventing approval processes
- Consequences: Reprimand, budget reduction, termination

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: What if I need to spend more than my budget allows?
A: Submit a budget transfer request or augmentation request with business justification to your manager and finance.

Q: Can I carry over unused budget to next year?
A: No, operating budgets do not roll over. Use-it-or-lose-it by December 31.

Q: How do I check my remaining budget?
A: Log into Adaptive Insights or request report from finance. Updated monthly by 5th business day.

Q: What if I'm tracking under budget?
A: This is favorable. Savings can be reallocated to higher priorities with approval, or drops to bottom line.

Q: When should I start planning for next year's budget?
A: Budget requests due in September. Start planning in August.

Q: Can I hire if I have budget for the headcount?
A: Yes, if approved headcount in budget. Still requires posting, interview process, and offer approval.

Q: What if business conditions change mid-year?
A: Submit reforecast explaining changes. May result in budget adjustments or cost reduction initiatives.

Q: How is my department's budget determined?
A: Based on strategic priorities, historical spending, headcount, and planned initiatives. Submitted by department, approved by exec team.
