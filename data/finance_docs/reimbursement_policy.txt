Employee Reimbursement Policy

Overview

This policy outlines the procedures for submitting and receiving reimbursement for approved business expenses. Employees are expected to spend company funds responsibly and submit accurate, timely expense reports.

Reimbursable Expenses

The following business expenses are eligible for reimbursement when properly documented and approved:

Travel Expenses:
- Airfare for business trips (economy class)
- Hotel accommodations at reasonable rates
- Ground transportation (rental cars, taxis, ride-sharing, parking)
- Mileage reimbursement for personal vehicle use at IRS standard rate
- Meals during business travel (within per diem limits)

Business Meals:
- Client entertainment and meals
- Team meals during extended work sessions
- Meals during required overtime or weekend work
- Conference and event meals not included in registration

Professional Development:
- Conference and seminar registration fees
- Professional certification exam fees
- Required training materials and textbooks
- Professional association membership dues (approved in advance)

Office and Supplies:
- Office supplies for remote workers
- Business cards
- Subscriptions to professional publications
- Software and tools required for job duties

Other Approved Expenses:
- Shipping and courier services
- Printing and copying for business purposes
- Phone charges for business calls while traveling
- Internet charges during business travel

Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are NOT eligible for reimbursement:

Personal Expenses:
- Personal entertainment or recreation
- Personal grooming (haircuts, spa services)
- Personal shopping or errands
- Clothing (except required safety equipment)
- Childcare or pet care
- Personal phone bills or personal calls

Travel-Related:
- First class or business class airfare (unless approved by VP+)
- Luxury hotel accommodations exceeding company limits
- Mini-bar charges
- In-room movies or entertainment
- Spouse or family travel expenses
- Commuting mileage between home and regular office

Fines and Penalties:
- Parking tickets
- Traffic violations
- Late fees due to personal delay
- Baggage fees for personal items

Other:
- Personal credit card annual fees or interest charges
- Tips exceeding standard guidelines (typically 15-20%)
- Alcoholic beverages (except during approved client entertainment)
- Political contributions
- Charitable donations (unless part of approved corporate giving)

Expense Submission Requirements

All expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of expense being incurred. Late submissions require manager approval and explanation.

Required Documentation:
- Original itemized receipts for all expenses over $25
- For meals: List of attendees and business purpose
- For mileage: Start/end locations, business purpose, odometer readings
- For conference/training: Agenda or program showing business relevance
- Credit card statements are NOT sufficient - itemized receipts required

Receipts should show:
- Vendor name and location
- Date of purchase
- Itemized list of goods/services
- Total amount paid
- Payment method

Lost Receipt Procedure:
If receipt is lost, submit expense with:
- Written explanation of lost receipt
- Description of expense and business purpose
- Best estimate of amount
- Manager approval required

Submission Process

Step 1: Complete Expense Report
- Use company expense management system (Expensify/Concur)
- Categorize expenses correctly using provided categories
- Attach digital copies of all receipts
- Provide business purpose for each expense
- Ensure mathematical accuracy

Step 2: Submit for Approval
- Submit to direct manager for review
- Manager reviews for policy compliance and business necessity
- Manager approves or rejects with comments
- Expenses over $500 require additional VP approval

Step 3: Finance Review
- Finance team reviews for completeness and compliance
- May request additional documentation or clarification
- Returns incomplete reports to employee for correction

Step 4: Reimbursement
- Approved expenses paid via direct deposit
- Payment processed within 7-10 business days of final approval
- Reimbursement appears on next regular payroll or separate ACH

Travel and Per Diem Guidelines

Airfare:
- Book economy class for domestic flights under 6 hours
- Book at least 14 days in advance when possible for best rates
- Use company preferred travel agency or booking tool
- Business class allowed for international flights over 6 hours (with VP approval)
- Baggage fees for one checked bag reimbursable

Hotels:
- Stay at reasonably priced hotels (not luxury properties)
- Typical limits: $150-200/night depending on city
- Higher cost cities (NYC, SF, LA): up to $250/night
- Extended stays (5+ nights): negotiate weekly rate
- Book hotels with free breakfast when possible

Meals and Per Diem:
Daily meal limits while traveling:
- Breakfast: $15
- Lunch: $20
- Dinner: $40
- Total daily maximum: $75

Tips should be reasonable (15-20% for meals, $5/bag for luggage).

Rental Cars:
- Rent mid-size or smaller vehicles
- Full-size vehicles allowed for 3+ passengers
- SUVs/luxury vehicles require VP approval
- Decline rental car insurance (company insurance covers)
- Refuel before returning to avoid inflated charges

Mileage Reimbursement:
- Current IRS standard rate: $0.655 per mile (updated annually)
- Only business miles reimbursed (not commuting)
- Submit mileage log with start/end addresses
- Cannot claim mileage if rental car used

Client Entertainment

Guidelines for business meals and entertainment:

Pre-Approval Required:
- Events over $200 per person
- Frequent entertainment of same client
- Entertainment involving alcohol
- Sporting events or concerts

Allowed Entertainment:
- Business meals with clients or prospects
- Networking events and conferences
- Client appreciation events
- Modest gifts for clients (under $50)

Documentation Requirements:
- List all attendees (names and companies)
- Describe business purpose and outcomes
- Itemized receipt required
- Alcohol must be noted separately if included

Corporate Credit Cards

Employees with corporate cards must:
- Use card only for approved business expenses
- Submit expense reports within 30 days
- Reconcile monthly statements
- Report lost/stolen cards immediately
- Never use for personal expenses

Personal expenses charged to corporate card:
- Must be reported immediately
- Must reimburse company within 7 days
- Repeated violations may result in card cancellation
- Serious violations subject to disciplinary action

Company will pay corporate card bills directly. Employee responsible for:
- Timely expense report submission
- Resolving any disputed charges
- Reimbursing company for personal charges

Compliance and Audits

Expense reports are subject to audit at any time. Finance and Internal Audit teams may:
- Request additional documentation
- Interview employees about expenses
- Review patterns and compliance
- Report policy violations

Policy violations may result in:
- Denial of reimbursement
- Requirement to repay company
- Written warning
- Suspension of travel privileges
- Termination of employment (for serious violations)

Common violations include:
- Inflating expenses or falsifying receipts
- Submitting personal expenses as business
- Claiming same expense twice
- Inappropriate or excessive spending
- Missing or inadequate documentation

Managers are responsible for:
- Reviewing expense reports carefully
- Ensuring policy compliance
- Questioning unusual or excessive expenses
- Setting good example with their own expenses

Special Situations

International Travel:
- Foreign currency expenses: Use exchange rate on date of transaction
- VAT/GST taxes: Reimbursable, company will seek refund where possible
- International phone/data charges: Reimbursable for business use
- Passport/visa fees: Reimbursable for business travel

Relocation Expenses:
- Covered under separate relocation policy
- Contact HR for relocation expense guidelines
- Pre-approval required for all relocation costs

Emergency Situations:
- Additional flexibility for genuine emergencies
- Contact manager and finance immediately
- Document circumstances thoroughly
- Will be reviewed case-by-case

Extended Business Travel (30+ days):
- Contact HR and Finance for extended travel guidelines
- May qualify for extended stay housing vs. hotels
- Different per diem rules may apply
- Tax implications for extended assignments
