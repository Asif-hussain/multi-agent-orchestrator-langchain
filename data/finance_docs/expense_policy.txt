CORPORATE EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT POLICY

OVERVIEW

This policy outlines eligible business expenses, approval requirements, and reimbursement procedures. All employees must comply with this policy when incurring business expenses on behalf of the company.

GENERAL PRINCIPLES

Business Purpose Requirement
All expenses must:
- Have a clear business purpose
- Be necessary and reasonable
- Be properly documented
- Comply with company policy
- Be submitted within 60 days of incurrence

Approval Requirements
- Expenses under $500: Manager approval
- Expenses $500-$2,000: Director approval
- Expenses over $2,000: VP approval
- All exceptions require CFO approval

TRAVEL EXPENSES

Airfare

Booking Requirements:
- Book through corporate travel portal (Concur Travel)
- Economy class for flights under 6 hours
- Premium economy allowed for flights 6+ hours with manager approval
- Business class requires VP approval
- First class not reimbursed except C-suite

Advance Booking:
- Book at least 14 days in advance when possible
- Last-minute bookings (under 7 days) require explanation
- Unused tickets should be credited for future use
- Cancellation fees may not be reimbursed without justification

Baggage Fees:
- One checked bag reimbursed for trips 3+ days
- Additional bags require justification
- Overweight fees generally not reimbursed
- Sports equipment for company events approved

Lodging

Hotel Guidelines:
- Book through corporate travel portal
- Standard rate limit: $200/night in major cities, $150/night elsewhere
- Extended stays (7+ nights): Negotiate weekly rate or consider corporate housing
- Preferred hotel chains: Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt (corporate rates available)
- Direct billing available for approved extended assignments

Incidental Charges:
- WiFi: Reimbursed if not complimentary
- Parking: Reimbursed with receipts
- Minibar: Not reimbursed
- Movies/entertainment: Not reimbursed
- Resort fees: Reimbursed if unavoidable
- Early check-in/late checkout: Not reimbursed without business justification

Alternative Accommodations:
- Airbnb/VRBO allowed for extended stays (7+ days) with pre-approval
- Must provide itemized receipt
- Should not exceed hotel rate limit
- Appropriate for business use (not vacation rentals)

Ground Transportation

Rideshare/Taxi:
- Uber/Lyft preferred for business travel
- Taxis acceptable when rideshare unavailable
- Airport transfers fully reimbursed
- UberX or equivalent standard (UberBlack requires justification)
- Submit itemized receipt showing trip details

Rental Cars:
- Compact or mid-size vehicles (standard rate)
- Full-size or SUV requires justification (multiple passengers, equipment)
- Book through corporate travel portal for insurance coverage
- Decline rental agency insurance (covered by corporate policy)
- Fuel: Fill tank before return or accept prepaid fuel option
- Tolls and parking: Reimbursed with receipts
- Luxury vehicles not reimbursed

Personal Vehicle Mileage:
- Reimbursement rate: IRS standard mileage rate (currently $0.67/mile for 2024)
- Submit mileage log with business purpose
- Parking and tolls reimbursed separately with receipts
- Maintenance and insurance not separately reimbursed

Public Transportation:
- Fully reimbursed (trains, subways, buses)
- Submit receipts or monthly pass documentation
- Corporate transit cards available for frequent travelers

Meals and Entertainment

Per Diem Rates

Meals while traveling:
- Breakfast: $15
- Lunch: $20
- Dinner: $40
- Daily total: $75

Per diem policies:
- Receipts required for meals over $25
- Alcohol limited to $25/day
- Cannot claim per diem if meals provided at conference/event
- Cannot claim per diem for meals with clients (use entertainment budget)

Client Entertainment:
- Must have clear business purpose
- Document attendees and business relationship
- Manager pre-approval for expenses over $200
- Reasonable and customary amounts
- Submit itemized receipt (not credit card slip)

Team Meals:
- Approved for team-building, working sessions, or celebrations
- Manager approval required
- $30/person limit for team lunches
- $50/person limit for team dinners
- Must include business purpose in expense report

Conference and Event Expenses

Registration Fees:
- Pre-approval required from manager
- Submit invoice and proof of payment
- Cancellation fees may not be reimbursed

Professional Development:
- Courses, certifications, conferences up to $2,000/year
- Submit course description and business justification
- Reimbursed upon proof of completion
- Materials and textbooks included

Trade Shows:
- Booth rental and materials approved with VP approval
- Marketing/promotional items approved in advance
- Client entertainment at events follows standard entertainment policy

OFFICE AND EQUIPMENT EXPENSES

Office Supplies:
- Pens, paper, notebooks: Up to $50/month
- Order through office supply portal when possible
- Specialized supplies require manager approval

Technology Equipment:
- Accessories (mouse, keyboard, headset): Up to $100 with manager approval
- Monitors: Through IT department only
- Personal laptop: Not reimbursed
- Software licenses: Through IT approval process
- Mobile phone accessories: Up to $50

Home Office (Remote Workers):
- One-time setup allowance: $500
- Desk, chair, lighting, accessories
- Internet reimbursement: Up to $75/month
- Annual refresh budget: $200
- Requires remote work agreement

TELECOMMUNICATIONS

Mobile Phone:
- Company-issued phones: All expenses covered
- BYOD (bring your own device): $75/month stipend
- International roaming: Pre-approval required
- Equipment upgrades: Every 24 months

Internet Service:
- Remote workers: Up to $75/month
- Must be business necessity
- Submit monthly bill with payment confirmation

MISCELLANEOUS EXPENSES

Parking:
- Business meetings: Fully reimbursed
- Office parking: Not reimbursed (use employee lot)
- Client site parking: Reimbursed
- Airport parking: Short-term for trips under 3 days, long-term for 3+ days

Business Materials:
- Books related to job function: Up to $100/year
- Professional subscriptions: Pre-approved
- Industry publications: Department budget

Shipping and Postage:
- Business mailings: Fully reimbursed
- Personal shipments: Not reimbursed
- Use corporate shipping accounts when available

REIMBURSEMENT PROCESS

Submitting Expense Reports

Timing:
- Submit within 60 days of expense date
- Expenses over 60 days may not be reimbursed
- Submit monthly for ongoing expenses
- Final expense report due within 30 days of employee departure

Required Documentation:
- Itemized receipt (credit card slip insufficient)
- Business purpose description
- Date and location
- Attendees (for meals/entertainment)
- Project/client code if applicable

Expense Report System:
1. Log into Concur Expense at concur.company.com
2. Create new expense report
3. Upload receipt images
4. Categorize each expense
5. Add business purpose
6. Submit for approval
7. Track approval status
8. Reimbursement via direct deposit within 10 business days

Credit Cards

Corporate Credit Cards:
- Available for employees with frequent business expenses
- Apply through finance department
- Personal liability for charges
- Payment due upon receipt of statement
- Expense report must be submitted before payment
- Late submission may result in card suspension

Personal Credit Cards:
- Use for business expenses if no corporate card
- Submit expense report for reimbursement
- Reimbursement processed within 10 business days
- Company not responsible for interest charges

NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES

The following are NOT reimbursed:
- Commuting to regular office location
- Personal entertainment (movies, sporting events)
- Minibar charges
- Personal grooming (haircuts, spa services)
- Gym memberships or fitness expenses
- Clothing (except required safety gear)
- Fines and penalties (parking tickets, traffic violations)
- Personal losses or theft
- Childcare or pet care
- Spouse/partner travel expenses (unless approved in advance for specific events)

POLICY VIOLATIONS

Consequences:
- First violation: Warning and required policy review
- Repeated violations: Denial of reimbursement
- Fraudulent expenses: Termination and potential legal action

Fraudulent Activity:
- Submitting false receipts
- Claiming personal expenses as business
- Duplicate reimbursement requests
- Inflating expense amounts

INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL

Additional Requirements:
- Pre-approval required 30 days in advance
- Passport and visa: Employee responsibility, costs reimbursed
- Travel insurance: Provided by company
- Currency exchange: Use corporate card for best rates
- VAT receipts: Obtain for reclaim purposes

Per Diem:
- Follows U.S. State Department rates by country
- Published at https://aoprals.state.gov/web920/per_diem.asp
- Includes lodging and meals
- Receipts required over $75

EXCEPTIONS AND SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES

Emergency Travel:
- Family emergency travel: Manager discretion
- Medical emergency: VP approval for exception
- Natural disaster/safety: CFO approval

Extended Assignments:
- Relocation expenses: Separate policy
- Temporary assignments (30+ days): Special per diem rates
- Corporate housing arranged by HR

Client-Billable Expenses:
- Follow client contract terms
- May differ from standard policy
- Document billable expenses separately
- Submit per client billing requirements
